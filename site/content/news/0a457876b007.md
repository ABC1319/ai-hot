Weeks after OpenAI rolled out a new family of conversational models and
updated ChatGPT’s voice mode
, rival Anthropic is making its move to make Claude more voice-friendly with a new update.

The company said Thursday that users can choose between Opus, Sonnet, and Haiku models.
Claude’s voice mode, which
was released last year
and powered by the Haiku model, provided quick responses, but wasn’t well suited for complex work.

The company said that with the new update, voice mode picks the last model people used in the text chat and uses its fastest version by default.
Anthropic said that the new voice mode can help users with longer conversations, including providing feedback on their communication style, talking through a pitch to a client, and brainstorming product market research.
