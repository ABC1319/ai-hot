OpenAI is
sunsetting
Atlas, the AI-powered browser it
launched in October
with ChatGPT at its core.

But it’s not giving up on the idea that AI should help people browse the web.

Instead, it’s taking some of the agentic browsing features it tested in Atlas and redistributing them across ChatGPT’s desktop app and a Google Chrome extension.
The move to shut down Atlas comes a few months after OpenAI’s CEO of applications Fidji Simo told the team to
cut back on “side quests,”
which led to the AI firm shutting down its AI
video-generation tool Sora
.
