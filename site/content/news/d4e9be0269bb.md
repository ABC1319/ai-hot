SK Hynix will be offering American depositary receipts (ADRs), a type of certificate that lets U.S.

investors buy a foreign stock without trading directly on an overseas exchange.

Each ADR will represent a tenth of a common share.

It is expected to price those securities on Thursday and begin trading on Friday.
Like Micron, SK Hynix is riding an AI-fueled boom credited to AI in both sales and stock price.

Its first-quarter revenues were up nearly 200% over the same quarter last year, it said, and its stock is up about 260% so far this year.

This is because systems that run AI are very memory intensive.
