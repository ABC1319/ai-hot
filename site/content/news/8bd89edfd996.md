Music streaming service
TIDAL
is the latest to take aim at AI-generated music with the introduction of
a new policy
that will prevent fully AI-generated music from making money on its platform.

In addition, TIDAL will use automated tools to remove AI-generated music that attempts to impersonate an artist or a group, the company said.
“We are committed to protecting and rewarding organic creativity to avoid compromising an artist’s ability to connect with and build their fandom from TIDAL subscribers.

Many have told us they do not want to be exposed to — or prompted to listen to — wholly AI-generated music,” wrote
Tony
Gervino
, TIDAL EVP and editor-in-chief, in an
announcement
