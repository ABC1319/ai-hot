Sperm donors need limits, says a European fertility group
Ties van der Meer doesn’t know how many siblings he has.

The 47-year-old was conceived at a private fertility clinic using sperm from an anonymous donor.

He eventually tracked down one sibling, but he may have others he’ll never find.
Other donor-conceived people have found they have tens or even hundreds of them.

“It does make you feel a bit mass-produced,” said one who discovered they had 25 half-siblings.
In response, a European fertility organization says we need international limits on the number of children a single donor can contribute to.
—Jessica Hamzelou
